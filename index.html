I am a Ph.D. candidate at Stevens Institute of Technology in ECE where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Shucheng Yu. Before that, I completed my first Ph.D. and B.Eng. in computer science from Renmin University of China. I was a visiting student at Indiana University of Bloomington.

My research builds systems to protect users' privacy and security with cryptography. Designing solutions at the rigorous level of security and with real-world scalability particularly motivates me, often through new cryptographic ideas. I am broadly interested in real-world security and privacy challenges.

I am expected to graduate in 2026 or earlier and looking for a PostDoc position!

Research Hightlights

  • Support users to search over tens of millions of strings ciphers in milliseconds, and enable compatibility with generic GIS\LBS applications via the GridSE system (USENIX'24).

    • Details --
    The most recent project I worked on, GridSE, for secure geographic search, is compatible with real-world GIS/LBS systems, which lets a client query a remote database server while hiding the query and data from the server. To reach this, we designed a new encryption primitive to identify keywords with the queried prefixes, extending it to satisfy the latest crypto norms (forward/backward privacy of searchable encryption).
