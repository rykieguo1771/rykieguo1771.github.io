Hi there! I am currently a second year Ph.D. student at the Schaefer School of Engineering and Science, Stevens Institute of Technology. I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Shucheng Yu. Before that, I received my first Ph.D. degree and my B.Eng. degree in Computer Science from Renmin University of China (RUC), and was a visiting student at Indiana University of Bloomington.

My research provides protection methods for preventing secure vulnerabilities in systems, defending attacks with the help of mordern cryptography. I am most interested in secure problems in real-world systems. I am open to research discussions and collaborations, feel free to get in touch!

I'll be expected in the job market 2025, looking for a PostDoc Position!

Research Hightlights

  • Support users to search over tens of millions of locations ciphers in miliseconds, and enable the compatibility with generic GIS\LBS applictions via GridSE (USENIX'24).

    • Details --
    The most resent project I worked on, GridSE, for secure grographic search, is compatible with real-world GIS/LBS systems, which lets a client query a remote database server while hiding the query and data from the server. To reach this, we design a new primitive to identify keywords with the queried prefixes and extend it to satisfy the securiety requirement of up-to-date crypto norms (forward/backward privacy of searchable encryption).
